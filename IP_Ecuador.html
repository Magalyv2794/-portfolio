Integrated Project: Arid Climate Case

Water governance and stakeholder organization of irrigation system in rio San francisco and rio minas subcatchment, Santa Isabel, Azuay, Ec.

Left & Right

The study areas for this project were focused on the San Francisco River and Minas River subcatchments, which comprise the Jubones River catchment, located in the Santa Isabel Canton that belongs to the Province of Azuay. This study uses DPSIR (Driver - Pressure - State - Impact - Response) framework to assess the management of the irrigation system in the study area. DPSIR is a framework that shows casual links between driving forces through pressures to states and impacts, and eventually, those will lead to responses. Drivers are defines as a need. Pressure is a result of meeting a need. State is a condition of an environment. Impact is a change in the physical, chemical, or biological state of the environment, and Response is actions taken by policymakers or societies in response to an undesired impact.

The data was collected by doing some interviews with the users during the fieldwork, and during a meeting with the Directive Board of Junta de Regantes, the main organization in charge of the irrigation's system management.
